How to Start a Small Garden: A Beginner’s Guide

July 15, 2025 · 820 words

Why start a garden? Growing your own vegetables saves money, connects you to nature, and gives you fresher produce. This guide walks step-by-step through planning, soil, planting, and maintenance.

1. Choose a spot

Pick a place with at least 4–6 hours of sunlight daily. If space is limited, use containers or vertical planters. Good drainage is essential; avoid areas where water pools.

2. Test and prepare soil

Loosen the soil and remove debris. Mix in compost to improve fertility. If the soil is poor, raised beds with a 50/50 mix of topsoil and compost work well.

3. Pick easy crops

For beginners, start with leafy greens (spinach, lettuce), herbs (basil, parsley), and fast-growing vegetables (radishes, bush beans). These give quick results and build confidence.

4. Water smart

Water early morning to reduce evaporation. Use a simple drip or soaker hose for consistency. Don’t overwater — moist but not waterlogged soil is best.

5. Maintain and harvest

Keep an eye for pests and remove weeds weekly. Harvest leaves and vegetables when young — harvesting often encourages more growth.

Growing your garden takes patience, but the steps above make it manageable even for busy beginners. Start small, learn, and expand each season.

Simple Daily Routines That Boost Productivity

August 2, 2025 · 600 words

Productivity isn't about working longer — it's about working smarter. These routines are easy to adopt and have a big impact.

Morning Routine (20 minutes)

Start with 5 minutes of stretching, 5 minutes of planning your top 3 tasks, and 10 minutes of focused work on the most important task. This sets the tone for the day.

Midday Reset (10 minutes)

Take a short break, drink water, and do a 5-minute walk. The reset helps sustain energy and focus for the afternoon.

Evening Review (10 minutes)

Quickly review what you completed and note 1–2 tasks for tomorrow. This reduces morning decision fatigue.

How to Prepare Groundnut (Peanut) for Sale: Postharvest Tips

October 5, 2025 · 680 words

Correct postharvest handling of groundnuts reduces losses and improves marketability.

Drying

Dry nuts on raised drying racks or clean tarpaulins. Avoid direct prolonged sunlight that may degrade quality. Dry to safe moisture levels to avoid mold.

Sorting and Storage

Sort out damaged nuts and store in clean, dry jute sacks in a cool, ventilated store to prevent aflatoxin and pest damage.
